How to Pick Your Lift

Now that you’ve decided you want to start working out, how exactly do you pick the lifts you want to do?

First off, you want to create a workout split. In a standard split, people will do push, pull, and leg days.

  • Push typically includes the chest, triceps, and shoulders. It’s called a “push day” as you do these exercises in a pushing motion.
  • Conversely, you have “pull” days which typically include the biceps and back muscles. These exercises include pulling motions.
  • Leg days can be split into two separate days (which is what I do) or done together, it truly is whatever your personal goals are. The two splits for legs are typically broken into hamstrings and glutes, then quadriceps and calves.

Secondly, you want to decide on how many sets, reps (repetition), and weight you want to do.

  • A typical amount of sets are 4-5

The reps you decide to do depend on your goals!

  • High reps low weight is good for building endurance and strength
  • Low reps high weight is good for building muscle mass
  • If you’re at a beginner level, high reps and low weight is a great starting point!
